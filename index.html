GEDANKENLYRIK

Der Gott der Stadt


Broadly he sits upon a block of stone.

The winds lie black around his mighty brow.

He glares in rage where, far and all alone,

The last few houses lose themselves somehow.

His red-streaked belly glows before the night,

The great cities kneel in awe at his feet.

The endless bells of churches reach his height,

Rising from black towers in waves complete.

Like Corybants, wild music shakes the air,

The roaring millions fill the streets in fright.

Factory smoke and chimneys' breath declare

Incense-like vapors curling through the night.

The brewing storm smolders in his deep brow,

The darkened evening drowns in silent dread.

Storms flutter, circling vultures watching now,

His bristling hair stands raging on his head.

He stretches forth his butcher’s fist so vast.

He shakes it hard. A sea of fire then speeds

Through narrow streets, a roaring furnace blast,

Devouring all, till morning light proceeds.


Georg Heym
